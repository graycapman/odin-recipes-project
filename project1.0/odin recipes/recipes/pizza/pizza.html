Neapolitan pizza, or pizza Napoletana, is known for its simple, high-quality ingredients and soft, chewy crust. The dough is made from just flour, water, salt, and yeast, then left to ferment slowly, which gives it its signature light texture and subtle flavor. After rising, the dough is stretched by hand into a thin round with a slightly thicker edge. The traditional toppings include crushed San Marzano tomatoes, fresh mozzarella (often mozzarella di bufala), a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil, and a few fresh basil leaves. Once assembled, the pizza is cooked at a very high temperature—typically in a wood-fired oven reaching about 430/480°C (800900°F). This quick bake, only about 60–90 seconds, creates the iconic leopard-spotted crust: soft and elastic inside with slight charring outside. The result is a fragrant, balanced pizza where the flavors of the fresh ingredients shine without being overshadowed by heavy sauces or toppings.

ingredients

For the dough:

  • flour (high-quality, finely milled)
  • Fresh or dry yeast
  • Salt
  • water
    •

    For the toppings:

  • tomatoes
  • Fresh mozzarella
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil

    • steps

    1. step

      Mix 500 g of 00 flour with 325 ml of water. Add 2 g dry yeast (or 5 g fresh yeast) and 15 g salt. Stir until it forms a rough dough

    2. step

      Knead by hand for 8/10 minutes until smooth and elastic.

    3. step

      Place the dough in a covered bowl and let it rise at room temperature for 2 hours.

    4. step

      Divide the dough into 3 or 4 balls (about 230/250 g each). Cover and let them rest.

    5. step

      Let the dough balls rise for 6/8 hours at room temperature (or overnight in the fridge)

    6. step

      Crush tomatoes by hand or use passata. Add a pinch of salt. Do not cook the sauce

    7. step

      Heat your oven as hot as possible (250–300°C / 480–570°F). If you have a pizza stone or steel, preheat it for at least 30 minutes

    8. step

      On a floured surface, press the dough from the center outward to form the crust. Do not use a rolling pin.

    9. step

      Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce, add sliced fresh mozzarella, a few basil leaves, and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil.

    10. step

      Bake on a stone/steel for 5–8 minutes, or in a wood-fired oven at 450–500°C for 60–90 seconds.

    11. step

      Add fresh basil, another drizzle of olive oil, and serve immediately.
